# PawLens Introduction — English Speaker Script

**Target length:** approximately 65 seconds

## Opening — before Slide 1 (0:00–0:12)

> I think life with dogs becomes more enjoyable and rewarding when we can better understand their signals and know how to respond. That is why I built PawLens: a ChatGPT app that helps guardians observe their dogs more carefully.

## Slide 1 — Why PawLens (0:12–0:22)

> This started with a familiar moment. The doorbell rings, a dog barks and steps back, and the owner has to decide what to do next. There is not enough information to label the dog, but there is enough to start observing carefully.

## Slide 2 — The gap we wanted to close (0:22–0:36)

> People often look for one answer: fear, aggression, or something else. But the useful details are smaller: where the dog moved, whether its posture changed, and whether it settles when the room gets quieter. PawLens turns those details into a short checklist.

## Slide 3 — Our point of view (0:36–0:51)

> We made two deliberate choices. PawLens does not write a dog's thoughts or give a diagnosis. And a model guess never becomes the dog's record. Possibilities stay provisional; only owner-confirmed observations can be saved.

## Slide 4 — From uncertainty to a calm next step (0:51–1:05)

> The flow is simple: describe the event, see what is known and missing, watch a few concrete cues, and take one calm action. I’ll now show that flow in ChatGPT.
