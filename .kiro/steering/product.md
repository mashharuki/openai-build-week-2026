# Product Overview

PawLens is a safety-first ChatGPT App that helps dog owners interpret a dog’s bark, optional image, immediate context, and confirmed observation history. It produces research-grounded hypotheses and safe next actions; it does not claim to translate a dog’s speech.

## Core Capabilities

- Turn multimodal input into a structured current assessment: possible state, confidence and limits, observable body-language cues, and a low-stimulation next action.
- Ask for additional context when confidence is low and direct concerning cases toward veterinary or behavior professionals.
- Maintain a dog-specific observation notebook by saving only owner-confirmed observations.
- Compare later encounters against confirmed observations, never treating an AI hypothesis as fact.

## Target Use Cases

The MVP serves Japanese urban dog owners who are unsure how to respond to everyday barking, starting with visitor and doorbell situations. A user supplies a guided description of the bark and situation, an optional posture image, and brief context, then uses the assessment to observe and choose a safer next step. A short bark recording is an auxiliary input enabled only where the runtime supports audio file input; the primary flow must complete without it.

## Value Proposition

PawLens is an interpreter of uncertainty: it helps people make better observations and empathetic decisions rather than presenting an unjustifiably certain emotional label. Its core trust boundary is the separation of temporary AI hypotheses from owner-confirmed facts.

## Product Boundaries

The MVP does not offer definitive dog-language translation, medical or behavioral diagnosis, hardware integration, continuous monitoring, or equivalent interpretation quality outside the visitor scenario. Reliable history comparison is scoped to two rounds in the same conversation.
